## Design Context

### Users
Israeli homebuyers reviewing contractor agreements before signing. Typically anxious, high-stakes decision: buying a new apartment from a developer. They are non-lawyers seeking professional protection. They use this site on desktop at home and on mobile when at the sales office. Context is stressful — they feel financially exposed and want to feel reassured by someone authoritative.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative. Protective. Refined.**

Not "cool startup." Not "government portal." Closer to: the partner at a serious law firm, the notary who signs your documents, the trusted advisor you call when the stakes are high. The interface should feel like receiving a handshake from someone who has done this a thousand times and will take care of it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light sections with a dramatic dark hero — the hero is cinematic (luxury house at golden hour), the content sections are clean and airy
- **Typography**: `Frank Ruhl Libre` (Hebrew-first serif, gravitas) for display headings; `Rubik` (clean humanist sans, great Hebrew legibility) for body. These pair like a legal letterhead with clean body copy.
- **Color**: Warm cream base tinted toward the brand green. Deep emerald for authority. Gold (amber-ochre) for trust and value signals. Never clinical white, never cold blue.
- **Anti-references**: Generic fintech dashboards. Anything with cyan gradients or purple glow. Generic card-icon-text grids that could be any SaaS.

### Design Principles
1. **Calm confidence over urgency** — No aggressive CTAs. Gold buttons signal value, not alarm. Spacing is generous; the site breathes.
2. **Authority through restraint** — One focal animation per section. Not every card floats; not every heading shimmers.
3. **Hebrew-first** — Every layout decision serves RTL. Pseudo-elements, gradients, and icon placement all account for right-to-left reading direction.
4. **Touch of luxury, not ostentation** — One glass card in the hero (the floating stats). One badge on the service photo. Not everywhere.
5. **Trust is earned through details** — WCAG AA contrast. Visible focus indicators. Consistent hover states. Empty states. Every form field labeled. These are the trust signals that matter.
